## The Big Realization: Intelligence Is About Seeing Relationships

Here's what I figured out: **Intelligence is basically how fast and clearly you can compare things.**

Think about it:
- Same vs different
- Before vs after  
- Bigger vs smaller
- Part vs whole
- Cause vs effect

Every hard IQ test question? It's just asking you to spot these relationships while your brain is bombarded with distractions and noise.

Smart people aren't magical—they're just really good at cutting through the chaos to see the real pattern underneath.

## Why Most Brain Training Fails (And What Actually Works)

Most brain games are too simple. They ask "Is this the same as before?" But real intelligence requires something deeper: **relational thinking**.

Instead of just recognizing "same" or "different," you need to actively compare HOW things relate:
- Is this position closer or farther?
- Is this sound higher or lower?
- Is this color the opposite?

That's when I had my breakthrough. I realized that every type of relationship—no matter how complex—can be broken down into one core skill: **comparing similarities and differences quickly and accurately**.

## The Game-Changer: My Relational N-Back Trainer

After understanding this, I worked with AI to create something new: a brain trainer that forces you to think relationally under pressure.

**Try it here:** [Relational N-Back - Dynamic Relation Buttons](https://find-1-bug.github.io/relational-n-back/)

### How it works:
1. You see dots, hear sounds, see colors and shapes
2. Each button shows a different relationship question (like "Closer?" or "Opposite?")
3. You only press if that exact relationship is true compared to N steps back
4. The game throws in "almost right" answers to train your brain to ignore distractions

It sounds simple, but it's incredibly challenging. You're training the exact skill that IQ tests measure.
