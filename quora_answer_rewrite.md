## The Big Discovery: Intelligence is About Seeing Patterns

Here's what I learned: Intelligence isn't about memorizing facts or having a "big brain." It's about **pattern recognition**—the ability to see order in chaos.

Think about it like this:

- **Easy IQ questions**: The pattern is obvious, like "2, 4, 6, 8... what comes next?"
- **Hard IQ questions**: You have to juggle multiple patterns at once—shapes, positions, movements, transformations—all while ignoring distractions.

The difference between average and genius intelligence? The genius can see the complex pattern as clearly as you see the simple one.

## The Secret: Truth vs. Noise

Ask yourself:
- What's actually true about this situation?

- What's the real pattern here?

This applies to everything:
- **Business**: What's the real market pattern vs. temporary noise?
- **Relationships**: What's genuine connection vs. surface-level interaction?
- **Learning**: What's the core concept vs. unnecessary details?

## The Practical Method: First Principles Thinking

## The Pattern Recognition Method

I developed a simple framework for improving fluid intelligence:

1. **Observe** the data around you
2. **Identify** the smallest repeating patterns
3. **Hold** these patterns in your working memory
4. **Connect** small patterns to form larger ones
5. **Compare** and contrast different patterns
6. **Stack** patterns until your working memory is full
7. **Sleep** to consolidate patterns into long-term memory
8. **Repeat** daily

After about 60 days of this practice, you develop a dense web of interconnected patterns. Your brain starts treating complex patterns as simple units—like upgrading from 8-bit to 64-bit processing.

## The Game-Changer: N-Back Training

This is where things get really interesting. **N-Back training** is a scientifically proven method for increasing working memory and processing speed.

### What is N-Back?
It's a brain training game where you have to remember something from N steps back and compare it to what's happening now. For example:
- **1-Back**: "Is this the same as what I saw 1 step ago?"
- **2-Back**: "Is this the same as what I saw 2 steps ago?"
- **3-Back**: "Is this the same as what I saw 3 steps ago?"

### Why It Works:
N-Back trains your brain to:
- Hold multiple pieces of information in working memory
- Compare them quickly and accurately
- Ignore distracting "lure" trials that look similar but aren't correct
- Process information faster under pressure

### The Evidence:
Dave Asprey (a well-known biohacker) increased his IQ by 18 points in just 20 sessions of N-Back training. His working memory went from holding 3 chunks of information to 5 chunks.

The math is compelling: **Each additional chunk of working memory correlates to about 10 IQ points**.

## My Personal Breakthrough: Relational N-Back

After years of studying intelligence, I had a major insight: **All forms of intelligence boil down to relational reasoning**—the ability to understand how things relate to each other.

Instead of just asking "Is this the same?" (like traditional N-Back), I wondered: **What if we trained the brain to recognize different types of relationships?**

This led me to create a more advanced training tool called **Relational N-Back**. Here's how it works:

### The Innovation:
- **Dynamic relational prompts**: Instead of just "same/different," the game asks questions like:
  - "Is the position bigger than N steps ago?"
  - "Is the color opposite to N steps ago?"
  - "Is the sound higher pitched than N steps ago?"
- **Multiple modalities**: Position, sound, color, and shape all at once
- **Interference control**: The game includes "lure" trials that look correct but aren't

### Why This Works Better:
Traditional N-Back only trains identity recognition ("same/different"). Relational N-Back trains your brain to:
- Recognize multiple types of relationships
- Switch between different relational criteria quickly
- Maintain multiple mental lists simultaneously
- Ignore distracting similar-but-wrong patterns
